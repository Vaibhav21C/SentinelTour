About Darjeeling

Discover the history, culture, and beauty of the Queen of Hills

The Queen of Hills

Nestled in the Lesser Himalayas at an elevation of 2,000 meters (6,700 ft), Darjeeling is internationally renowned for its tea industry and the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The name Darjeeling comes from the Tibetan words "Dorje" meaning thunderbolt and "ling" meaning place or land, hence "the land of the thunderbolt".

The history of Darjeeling is intertwined with that of Nepal, Sikkim, Bhutan, and Bengal. Until the early 19th century, the area was ruled by the Kingdom of Sikkim, with settlement consisting of a few villages of the Lepcha people.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Darjeeling is a melting pot of different cultures including Nepali, Tibetan, Bengali, and indigenous Lepcha traditions.

World-Famous Tea

Known as the "Champagne of Teas," Darjeeling tea is renowned worldwide for its muscatel flavor and exquisite aroma.

Himalayan Railway

The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, also known as the "Toy Train," is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that has been operating since 1881.
